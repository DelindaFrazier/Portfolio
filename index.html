American Association of Community Colleges

  • Role: Content Manager
  • Software:
    Adobe CC: Dreamweaver and Photoshop, SharePoint CMS,

AACC is a membership organization and the primary advocacy organization for America’s community colleges. The AACC website keeps its members informed by providing information on conferences and events, publications, training opportunities, related legislative issues and grant opportunities. The website also provides information to the general public about the advantages of attending community colleges.

I was employed by the association for 14 years, during which, I witnessed and participated in the evolution of the Association's Web presence. In 2001, I used FrontPage and Photoshop to design and develop the Association's first event websites. When the association’s Web presence and moved to SharePoint, I continued to manage event content and assisted in developing content strategy.

When I left the Association in 2014, I was responsible for the development and maintenance of four public-facing event sub sites, one intranet sub site and two mobile apps. For these sites I wrote, edited and posted content, created and manipulated graphics and images, met with meeting managers to gather event website requirements and managed external databases that fed into the sub sites.

U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission

Previous Next
  • Type: Content Management, Web Development
  • Role: Content Manager
  • Software: Oracle Webcenter Sites CMS, Notepad++ Text Editor
    Adobe CC: Dreamweaver, Photoshop

Securities and Exchange Commission is an agency of the United States government tasked with enforcing federal securities laws and regulating the securities industries. Its website provides the public information about investing, existing securities regulations, proposed securities regulations and enforcement activities.

I work with a five person team to maintain the SEC website. Some of my responsibilities include using hand-coded HTML, CSS to publish regulatory and enforcement documents; cleaning and transferring content from legacy pages to the new template; rand reviewing and resolving 508 Compliance issues and redacting personally identifiable information from documents before publishing; and responding to staff requests for content updates.

Only half of the SEC website resides within the CMS. The other pages are skinned with templates built on the 960 Grid System. These pages are not responsive and need to be moved into a new template.

As a side project, I have been experimenting with recreating the current SEC webpage template in different responsive frameworks. So far, I have recreated the template in Bootstrap. I plan to also use Foundation and the new U.S. Web Design Standards for federal websites proposed by 18F.

Article: Eight Pieces of Public Art Worth Seeing

Previous Next
  • Type: Multimedia, Web Development
  • Role: Designer, Developer, Photographer
  • Software: Dreamweaver, Photoshop
  • Technologies: HTML, CSS, jQuery, Bootstrap

This is my capstone project for the Interactive Journalism masters program. The assignment was to report on a particular topic then create a multimedia presentation using the skills learned over the course of the program to further explore and enhance the reporting.

I was inspired to focus on public art in the District of Columbia after working with a dataset on the same topic during our unit on data visualization. For my presentation, I decided to focus on pieces of public art that I had driven past my entire life, but had never taken the time to explore.

I used Bootstrap for the framework of the website and the jQuery plugin, Fancybox, for the pop-up windows. The majority of the Images were captured by me with my Cannon EOS Rebel T3i DSLR and edited with Photoshop. Audio was captured with a Zoom H1 Handy Portable Digital Recorder and edited with Adobe Audition CC.

One Day at a Time (Slideshow with audio)

  • Type: Multimedia
  • Role: Producer
  • Software: Adobe CC: Photoshop, Audition and Premiere Pro
  • Equipment: Cannon EOS Rebel T3i DSLR, Zoom H1 Portable Digital Recorder

This slideshow was submitted as the final project for the class Visual Storytelling, which was part of the Interactive Journalism masters program. The goal of the project was to use photography and audio to tell a reported story.

The subject of my project is Michelle G., a single mother of two who is pursuing a masters in social work while working full time. To collect material for the project, I spent a weekend shadowing, interviewing and photographing Michelle and her family.

I used Photoshop to edit the photos, Adobe Audition to edit the audio and Adobe Premiere Pro to sync the audio and images. All images were captured by me with my Cannon EOS Rebel T3i DSLR. Audio was captured with a Zoom H1 Portable Digital Recorder.
